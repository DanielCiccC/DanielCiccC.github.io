Timing in the market

  • Determine the best exit timing based upon the payoffs of the futures contract

Determine the payoff for every time period T. Here is where we will investigate the payoffs for a couple of given contracts that would have been available during this time, i.e. ESH20 and ESM20, with maturities in March and June, respectively.

The open prices for these contracts were sourced from BarChart Premiere

Firstly, we will graphically illustrate the payoffs of the purchasing the contracts starting from the evaluation date to the contract maturity, i.e. 31st March and 30th June.

We will characterise the best exit timing of these securities as the date which would yield the maximum payoff between the evaluation date and maturity.

# Retrieve historic futures data for ESH20 and ESM20 - two futures would be relevant to the timing of this 

def generate_futures_payoffs(historical_prices_wd, contract_size):
    
    futures_prices_df = pd.read_csv(historical_prices_wd, index_col='Time', parse_dates=['Time'])['Open'].to_frame()
    futures_prices_df = futures_prices_df[futures_prices_df.index >= EVAL_DATE]
    futures_prices_df.index = pd.to_datetime(futures_prices_df.index).strftime('%Y-%m-%d')
    futures_prices_df = futures_prices_df.sort_index()
    futures_prices_df = futures_prices_df.rename(columns={'Open': 'Price'})

    esh20_eval_date = futures_prices_df['Price'].loc[EVAL_DATE]
    # esm20_eval_date = futures_prices_df.loc[EVAL_DATE]

    futures_prices_df['Payoff from EVAL_DATE'] = (esh20_eval_date - futures_prices_df['Price']) * (H * contract_size)
    return futures_prices_df

esh20_prices_df = generate_futures_payoffs(ESH20_HIST_PRICES, ESH20_CONTRACT_SIZE)
esm20_prices_df = generate_futures_payoffs(ESM20_HIST_PRICES, ESM20_CONTRACT_SIZE)
